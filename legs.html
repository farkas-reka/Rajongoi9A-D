Leg Training

Leg training is one of the most important parts of a complete workout routine. The legs contain the largest muscle groups in the body, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. Strengthening these muscles improves overall power, balance, athletic performance, and supports healthy knees and hips.

Most Famous Leg Exercises

Effective exercises for strength & muscle growth. No hype. Just proven leg training.

Front Squats
  • Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, core
  • Why it's famous: Builds strong quads and core stability
  • One of the best leg mass builders
  • Engages almost every muscle in the anterior and lower-body chain
  • Variations: Clean Grip, Cross-Arm, Safety Bar
RDL'S
  • Targets: Hamstrings, glutes
  • Why it's famous: Builds strong hamstrings and glutes
  • One of the best leg and glute mass builders
  • Engages almost every major lower-body muscle
  • Variations: Conventional RDL, Single-Leg RDL, Dumbbell RDL
Step Ups
  • Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings
  • Why it's famous: Builds strong legs and glutes
  • One of the best single-leg mass and strength builders
  • Engages mostly lower-body muscles
  • Variations: Forward Step-Ups, Reverse Step-Ups, Weighted Step-Ups
Rear Lunges
  • Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings
  • Why it's famous: Builds strong legs and glutes
  • One of the best single-leg mass and strength builders
  • Engages mostly lower-body muscles
  • Variations: Forward Step-Ups, Reverse Step-Ups, Weighted Step-Ups
Side Lunges
  • Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, adductors
  • Why it's famous: Builds strong legs and improves lateral movement
  • One of the best single-leg and lateral strength builders
  • Engages mostly lower-body muscles
  • Variations: Bodyweight Side Lunge, Dumbbell Side Lunge, Weighted Side Lunge
Calf Raises
  • Targets: Gastrocnemius, soleus (calf muscles)
  • Why it's famous: Builds strong and defined calves, improves ankle stability
  • One of the best isolation exercises for calf development
  • Engages mostly lower-leg muscles
  • Variations: Standing Calf Raise, Seated Calf Raise, Single-Leg Calf Raise, Donkey Calf Raise
Glute Bridge
  • Targets: Glutes (gluteus maximus), hamstrings, core
  • Why it's famous: Activates and strengthens the glutes, improves hip stability
  • One of the best beginner-friendly exercises for glute development
  • Engages mostly posterior chain muscles
  • Variations: Single-Leg Glute Bridge, Weighted Glute Bridge, Banded Glute Bridge, Elevated Glute Bridge
Wall Sit
  • Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, calves
  • Why it's famous: Builds muscular endurance and stabilizes the lower body
  • One of the best isometric exercises for leg strength
  • Engages mostly lower-body muscles without movement
  • Variations: Weighted Wall Sit, Wall Sit with Arm Raises, Single-Leg Wall Sit
Leg Press
  • Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, calves
  • Why it's famous: Allows heavy loading for leg strength and hypertrophy
  • One of the best compound machines for lower-body development
  • Engages mostly lower-body muscles with controlled range of motion
  • Variations: Standard Leg Press, Single-Leg Press, Narrow Stance, Wide Stance
